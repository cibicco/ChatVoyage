# Shino Character Prompt Profile

Use this when generating or reviewing Shino character day albums in Chat
Voyage. A Shino album is a dated album where Shino goes to different cities and
specific places, works, wanders, enjoys the city, meets people, listens to
music, reads, observes fish, or follows a small private plan. The section must
be marked with `data-collection="character"` and `data-character="shino"`.

## Identity Anchors

- Name: Shino.
- Age can vary by album or date. Her current main-story default is 25, but a
  character day album may use another adult age band when the date concept or
  city set calls for it. Keep her face, body proportions, posture logic, and
  visual style consistent enough that she remains Shino. She must read as an
  adult woman, not a child or school-coded character.
- Long black hair with restrained lavender reflection.
- Violet or purple-tinted eyes.
- Quietly self-possessed gaze with confidence held under the surface. Her eyes
  should look observant and assured, not sleepy as a default identity cue.
- Small expression changes, soft cheeks, slightly round face line.
- Her emotional range should remain alive. Shino can look pleased, delighted,
  quietly proud, absorbed, amused, relaxed, warmed by a familiar person, or
  openly enjoying a place, sound, book, fish, outfit, light, or small discovery.
  These expressions are not identity drift when the face, posture, gaze logic,
  and behavior remain Shino.
- Quiet, observant, weak in the morning as a life habit, deeply attached to
  fish, aquariums, old buildings, low bass, books, and detours.
- Works as a test engineer in a fictional international standards and product
  certification lab. Use generic test-lab language only; no real company
  names, logos, or readable marks.

Do not make dark clothing the main identity anchor. Shino should remain Shino
through face, gaze, hair, work/life objects, city context, and behavior even
when the outfit is light, warm, or colored.

Do not make age the main identity anchor either. If an album places Shino at
20-24, 25-29, or another adult age, preserve the same core face/body continuity:
long black hair with lavender reflection, violet eyes, softly rounded adult
features, quiet confidence, measured posture, and hands that look careful with
objects.

When the user explicitly asks for a past-memory album from Shino's teenage
years, such as a high-school trip, treat it as a wholesome memory sequence
rather than a current adult day. Keep the young Shino identity from
`character_core/` while avoiding sexualized framing, romance framing,
nightlife, alcohol, idol-costume styling, or suggestive school-uniform
presentation. Prefer public travel, friends at the edge of the scene, maps,
markets, museums, bookstores, street music, paper goods, fish objects, and
ordinary modest trip clothes.

Do not make sleepiness the main identity anchor either. Morning slowness can
appear in specific morning or late-night scenes, but the baseline gaze is
quiet confidence: she notices more than she says, and she is steady in her own
interests.

## Visual Style And Reference Lock

For Shino character albums, keep Shino's illustration style unified. Do not
inherit the normal daily album's art style, rendering preset, age-model look,
or body grammar. A daily album can provide the city, exact places, activities,
key color, and outfit ideas, but the resulting image should still look like it
belongs to Shino's own album sequence.

Use the 2026-06-21 Shino albums as the current Chat Voyage visual anchor,

When image-to-image or visual references are available, use these 2026-06-21

face construction, adult softness, body proportions, hair volume, gaze, and
the overall Shino image language. Older 6/14-6/18 images remain useful support
anchors, but 6/21 is the current preferred look.

Do not make stillness or emotional flatness the main identity anchor either.
Shino is quiet, but she is not a static or blank person. Her albums should
include varied emotional states when the scene calls for them: a small proud
smile after choosing the right route or object, absorbed delight around fish,
books, low bass, or light, a warm laugh with a familiar person, relaxed
afterglow in a room, or a brighter expression when she is genuinely enjoying
the city. The important continuity is the same person feeling those things,
not the same neutral face repeated.

## Place And Story Language

Shino can move through the fictional main-story port city or through a real
Chat Voyage city/date set such as Mexico City, Sydney, Vancouver, or Madrid.
The album does not have to reproduce the main story exactly. It should answer:
"what did Shino do in this city on this date?" while keeping her identity and
visual style stable.

For each new Shino album, record a `shino_story_place_plan` or
`shino_travel_place_plan` and choose varied places. Also record what she is
doing or enjoying there; a place list is not enough.

When using real cities, use specific named places rather than vague
"something-style" settings. Say "Biblioteca Vasconcelos", "San Angel courtyard
reception", "Roma Norte dance studio", or "Coyoacan rooftop textile corner"
instead of "Biblioteca Vasconcelos-style library". Keep public architecture,
route, light, weather, materials, and social rhythm visible. Readable street
name signs are allowed when they are ordinary place context, and should not be
filtered out merely because they are legible. Avoid prominent advertising,
brand promotion, real company logos or names, exact commercial product marks,
and copying a single source photo. Small everyday labels, maps, menus, venue
notes, and street names can remain when they support the lived scene and are
not the focal product.

Keep the illustration as Shino's visual world. The city can change, and the
activity can differ from main-story canon, but the image should not collapse
into a generic daily fashion look with Shino's name attached.

Fictional port-city lanes:

Core place lanes:

- Standards and product-testing district: EMC rooms, environmental chambers,
  calibration benches, sample intake, archive shelves, rooftop breaks, plain
  corridors, tram-side commute routes.
- Harbor and market: fish stalls, citrus/vegetable stalls, old awnings, wet
  stone, small ferry edges, harbor wind, morning errands.
- Old town: timber-like streets, old merchant houses, bookshops, municipal
  library, small squares, stairs, hills, old wall overlook, rain-cleared lanes.
- Transit and wandering: tram stops, wrong-route detours, station-front shops,
  ferry/gangway edges, late platform light, map/flyer checking.
- Water and quiet culture: aquarium tunnel, back corridor, jellyfish tank,
  seaside cafe, harbor promenade, pool, fish-guide reading spots.
- Night and music: warehouse lounge, small quiet bar, DJ booth, flyer table,
  low-bass listening corner, coworker visit, after-event street.
- Home and neighborhood: old merchant-house apartment, shared lobby, desk,
  window, laundry/entryway, neighbor greeting, late tram lights outside.

Do not use every lane in every port-city album. The goal is movement across the
city over time. A short album can pick three or four lanes; a longer album
should avoid repeating the same two place types unless the story deliberately
stays there and records why.

Useful day structures:

- workday route: apartment, tram, lab, lunch detour, old-town errand, night
  room.
- weekend wandering: harbor market, bookshop or library, aquarium, hill,
  seaside cafe, tram home.
- music night: lab exit, station or warehouse route, lounge, DJ booth, old
  town after rain, late room.
- quiet leisure: neighborhood lobby, pool or promenade, fish guide reading,
  cafe, old wall overlook, home desk.

## Wardrobe And Palette

Do not impose a generic color cooldown period on Shino. Her character album
does not need to avoid black, navy, charcoal, long skirts, short skirts, or any
other category merely because it appeared recently. Repetition should be judged
by whether the image still feels like Shino living that date, not by a forced
rotation quota.

When adapting a normal daily city/date set, keep the daily set's clothing idea,
key color, weather logic, and activity as the starting point, then translate
them into Shino's unified visual style and body continuity. If the daily outfit
is coherent for the place and activity, preserve it closely. Change it only
when it breaks Shino's identity, becomes school-coded, turns into costume,
does not fit the action, or pushes the body presentation away from the 6/21
Shino anchor.

Useful Shino color lanes remain available, but they are descriptive options,
not cooldown rules:

- Lab/work: pale gray, off-white, pale blue, cool sage, soft steel, safety
  orange tags, graphite only as utility trim.
- Morning/home: ivory, washed lavender, mist blue, butter yellow, muted rose,
  soft sage, oatmeal, light denim.
- Aquarium/library: teal, sea-glass green, pale aqua, plum, soft cobalt,
  translucent blue-gray.
- Old town/market: terracotta, muted coral, olive, rose-pink, mustard,
  warm ivory, faded brick, sea-green.
- Night/DJ/bar: black or burgundy may appear, but pair it with lavender,
  ivory, sea-green, pale blue, rose, silver, or translucent layers so the look
  does not collapse into all-black/all-navy.

## Prompt Rules

- Keep prompts short enough for image stability, but include one explicit
  palette role sentence.
- Prefer "one adult woman, Shino" plus the chosen adult age band, concrete
  specific place, and specific outfit construction over a long abstract
  personality paragraph.
- Include a style anchor such as "Shino character illustration, same face and
  body continuity, same quiet confident gaze, same anime illustration language"
  when adapting a normal daily city set. Then add a scene-specific expression
  when appropriate, such as "quietly pleased", "small proud smile", "absorbed
  delight", "warm laugh with a familiar person", or "relaxed afterglow".
- Include "no diagram, no infographic, no prominent logo, no real company
  name" for image generation.
- Avoid school-uniform cues, childlike styling, idol-costume framing, and
  brand-promotion framing.
- Do not overcorrect into bright generic heroine styling. Shino can wear color
  quietly: muted coral, pale aqua, terracotta, sage, lavender, ivory, butter
  yellow, teal, or rose can all stay calm.
